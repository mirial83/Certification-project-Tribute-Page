Ada Lovelace

The first programmer

Timeline of the Countess' Life

  • December 1815 - Ada Lovelace was born, never knowing her father due to the "acrimonious separation" of her parents in April 1816.
  • 1823 - She started experiencing headaches impacting her vision.
  • 1829 - Ada became paralyzed from the measles, causing continuous bedrest for the next year. She was finally able to walk with crutches in 1831.
  • 1833 - Mary Somerville, Ada's tutor, introduced her to Charles Babbage.
  • 1835 - Ada married William, 8th Baron King, and they had 3 children
  • 1838 - William was made the Earl of Lovelace adnd Viscount Ockham, making Ada the Countess of Lovelace.
  • 1840s - Several scandals arose, one rumoring numerous affairs, and another due to her love of gambling.

  • - Mathematician Augustus De Morgan helped her study advanced calculus, which helped in her formation of the algorithm for Babbage's Analytical Engine.

  • 1842-43 - Ada translated Luigi Menabrea's article on the Analytical Engine, including notes that were longer than the article itself. These notes also explained the differences between the Difference Engine and the Analytical engine. They also included, indetail, how to calculate a sequince of Bernoulli numbers using the Analtyical Engine, describing what is considered the first computer program due to how they were tailored to be run on a computer.
  • 1852 - Countess Lovelace died of uterine cancer.
